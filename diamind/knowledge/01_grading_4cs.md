# Diamond Grading: The 4Cs

The 4Cs — Color, Clarity, Cut, and Carat weight — are the universal framework developed by GIA (Gemological Institute of America) for describing diamond quality. Every certified diamond is graded on all four dimensions.

---

## Color

Color measures the absence of color in a white (colorless) diamond. Graded D through Z.

| Grade | Category | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| D | Colorless | Absolutely colorless. Rarest and most valuable. |
| E | Colorless | Minute traces of color detectable only by expert gemologists. |
| F | Colorless | Slight color visible only to expert under controlled conditions. |
| G | Near Colorless | Color noticeable when compared to higher grades but appears colorless face-up. Strong value grade. |
| H | Near Colorless | Similar to G; slight warmth visible in larger stones. Industry sweet spot for price/appearance. |
| I | Near Colorless | Slightly detectable warmth. Often undetectable face-up in smaller stones. |
| J | Near Colorless | Faint warmth visible. Noticeable in larger stones (>1ct). |
| K–M | Faint | Faint yellow tint visible to untrained eye. |
| N–R | Very Light | Light yellow or brown clearly visible. |
| S–Z | Light | Very obvious yellow or brown. |

**Pricing implication**: Each color grade step has a significant price impact. D-F command the highest premiums. G-H represent the best value for eye-clean appearance. Crossing the D-E-F to G or the J to K boundary causes significant price drops.

**Fancy colors** (natural pink, blue, yellow, green, etc.) fall outside the D-Z scale and are graded separately. They command extreme premiums. These are NOT present in this dataset.

## Clarity

Clarity measures the absence of inclusions (internal) and blemishes (surface). Graded on an 11-point scale.

| Grade | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| FL | Flawless | No inclusions or blemishes visible under 10x magnification. Extremely rare. |
| IF | Internally Flawless | No inclusions; only minor blemishes visible under 10x. |
| VVS1 | Very Very Slightly Included 1 | Inclusions so minor they are difficult for a skilled grader to see under 10x. |
| VVS2 | Very Very Slightly Included 2 | Inclusions very difficult to see under 10x. |
| VS1 | Very Slightly Included 1 | Minor inclusions difficult to see under 10x. |
| VS2 | Very Slightly Included 2 | Minor inclusions somewhat easy to see under 10x. |
| SI1 | Slightly Included 1 | Noticeable inclusions easy to see under 10x; typically eye-clean. |
| SI2 | Slightly Included 2 | Very noticeable under 10x; may be visible to the naked eye. |
| I1 | Included 1 | Inclusions visible to the naked eye; may affect brilliance. |
| I2 | Included 2 | Obvious inclusions visible to naked eye; affect brilliance. |
| I3 | Included 3 | Prominent inclusions affecting brilliance and possibly durability. |

**Eye-clean** means no inclusions visible to the naked eye in normal viewing conditions. Most SI1 and better diamonds are eye-clean. Some SI2 can be eye-clean depending on inclusion type and position.

**Pricing implication**: FL and IF carry significant premiums. VVS1-VVS2 are top-tier. VS1-VS2 are excellent value. SI1 is the most popular budget-conscious choice. SI2 and below are heavily discounted.

## Cut

Cut refers to how well a diamond's facets interact with light — specifically its proportions, symmetry, and polish. Applies primarily to Round Brilliant Cuts. Cut grade is the most impactful factor in brilliance, fire, and scintillation.

| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| EX (Excellent) | Maximum brightness, fire, and scintillation. Superior craftsmanship. |
| VG (Very Good) | High brightness; minor deviations from ideal proportions. |
| GD (Good) | Reflects most light. Some compromises in proportions. |
| FR (Fair) | Less bright; significant proportion deviations. |
| PR (Poor) | Poor light return. Not commercially desirable. |

**Triple Excellent (EX/EX/EX)**: A diamond graded Excellent in Cut, Polish, AND Symmetry. Commands a premium and is the most commercially sought-after grade in the industry. Also written as 3EX.

**Cut applies only to Round Brilliant Cuts** in GIA grading. For fancy shapes (Emerald, Princess, Oval, etc.), only Polish and Symmetry are graded — there is no overall Cut grade.

---

## Polish

Polish measures the smoothness of each facet's surface after cutting and polishing.

| Grade | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| EX (Excellent) | No polish features visible under 10x |
| VG (Very Good) | Very difficult to see under 10x |
| GD (Good) | Difficult to see under 10x |
| FR (Fair) | Easy to see under 10x; visible to naked eye |
| PR (Poor) | Very obvious to naked eye |

---

## Symmetry

Symmetry measures the exactness of the diamond's shape and arrangement of facets.

| Grade | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| EX (Excellent) | No symmetry features visible under 10x |
| VG (Very Good) | Very difficult to see under 10x |
| GD (Good) | Difficult to see under 10x |
| FR (Fair) | Easy to see under 10x |
| PR (Poor) | Very obvious under 10x |

---

## Carat Weight

Carat (ct) is the unit of weight for diamonds. 1 carat = 0.2 grams = 100 points.

**Key weight thresholds** (magic sizes):
- 0.50 ct (half carat)
- 0.75 ct (three-quarter)
- 1.00 ct (one carat) — largest price-per-carat jump
- 1.50 ct
- 2.00 ct
- 3.00 ct
- 5.00 ct+

Diamonds just below a magic size (e.g., 0.98ct, 1.48ct) are significantly cheaper per carat than those just above it. This is because buyers pay a premium to hit the psychological thresholds.

**Size vs. appearance**: A well-cut Round Brilliant 1ct diamond measures approximately 6.5mm in diameter. Face-up appearance is also influenced by cut — a well-cut stone appears larger than a deep, poorly cut stone of the same weight.
