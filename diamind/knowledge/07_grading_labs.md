# Diamond Grading Laboratories

Grading laboratories certify diamonds by issuing grading reports that document the 4Cs, proportions, fluorescence, and other characteristics. The lab that issued the certificate directly affects a diamond's market value and buyer confidence.

---

## GIA — Gemological Institute of America

**Industry standard. The most trusted and consistent grading lab globally.**

- Founded in 1931; non-profit educational institution.
- Gold standard for Round Brilliant Cut grading including full Cut grade.
- Reports are universally accepted by all buyers, dealers, and auction houses.
- GIA consistently grades to a strict standard — no grade inflation.
- GIA diamonds command a **premium** over equivalent grades from other labs.
- GIA issues the "GIA Diamond Grading Report" (for diamonds ≥0.15ct) and "GIA Diamond Dossier" (for smaller stones with fewer plotted details).

**Key fact**: GIA invented the 4Cs framework and the D-Z color scale.

Certificate number format: 10-digit numeric (e.g., `2526030037`). Can be verified at gia.edu.

---

## IGI — International Gemological Institute

- Founded in 1975; headquartered in Antwerp, with labs globally.
- The largest independent gemological laboratory.
- Commonly used for lab-grown diamonds.
- Historically graded somewhat more generously than GIA (a VS1 from IGI might be VS2 by GIA standards). The gap has narrowed in recent years.
- IGI diamonds typically trade at a **5–15% discount** to GIA equivalents for natural diamonds.
- Certificate number: typically 9–10 digits. Can be verified at igi.org.

---

## HRD — Hoge Raad voor Diamant (Diamond High Council)

- Belgium-based; founded in 1973.
- Respected European lab, primarily used in Antwerp and European markets.
- Grading standards close to GIA; slight premium in European trade.
- Less common in Indian market and Asian trade.

---

## EGL — European Gemological Laboratory

- Multiple independent franchises globally (EGL USA, EGL International, etc.).
- **Significantly more generous** grading than GIA — a stone graded G/VS2 by EGL might be I/SI2 by GIA.
- EGL-certified diamonds trade at substantial discounts (sometimes 20–40% below GIA for equivalent reported grades).
- Many dealers and auction houses will not accept EGL reports for high-value stones.
- EGL USA and EGL International are separate entities with different (inconsistent) standards.

---

## AGS — American Gem Society

- Founded 1934; non-profit U.S.-based lab.
- Uses 0–10 grading scale (0 = best) in addition to GIA-like descriptors.
- Known for very strict cut grading — some consider AGS Ideal (0) the finest cut standard.
- Less commonly seen in the market than GIA.

---

## None / Uncertified

Some diamonds in the dataset (particularly Karigar/melee goods) have no lab certification. These are:
- Small melee diamonds sold in parcels where certification costs more than stone value.
- Goods where the supplier assesses quality internally.
- Uncertified diamonds are typically discounted vs. certified equivalents — buyer relies entirely on the dealer's representation of quality.

## Lab Comparison at a Glance

| Lab | Trust Level | Notes |
|-----|-------------|-------|
| GIA | Gold standard | Most consistent; commands premium |
| HRD | Very high | Top European lab |
| AGS | High | Strict cut standards |
| IGI | Good | Slight grade inflation historically; widely used for lab-grown |
| EGL | Lower | Significant grade inflation; large discount |
| None | Varies | Depends entirely on supplier credibility |

## Impact on This Dataset

All Glowstar and Vaibhav stones are GIA-certified. Ratnakala includes a mix of GIA and some uncertified (NaN lab field). Zhaveri is predominantly GIA. The lab field should always be considered when comparing prices across stones — a GIA G/VS1 and an IGI G/VS1 are not equivalent in market value.
